Project Reflect

Posted on August 20, 2021

It took a while (mostly because of procrastination) but I’ve finally got a prototype of a simple bullet hell game where you can reflect (almost) everything that I call “Project Reflect” (working title).

Originally, I planned for a bullet hell game with a gimmick, so I based the game on this reflect mechanic. So far, it only reflects one type of bullet and pushes enemies, but I plan to add more interactions later.

Here’s what it looks like so far:
I wanted gameplay to be simple so the controls are also simple:

       WASD: Movement
 Left Click: Reflect (2 charges)
Right Click: Dash (not shown in gif)


When I tried deciding on a theme for the game, I somehow ended up with hololive (specifically Ayame) because that was what I was mostly interested in at the time. I admit I’m too far in the rabbithole to leave. With that said, the (rough) BGM is based on the songs Ayame have been a part of (or in other words, a Suspect and Hyakkaryouran Hanafubuki chiptune mashup):

There’s a lot I plan to change later in development, like replacing the health bar with “hearts”, updating the UI/font, adding a crosshair and other stuff. It’s also kinda boring right now with just one enemy and one bullet type, but I plan to add more enemies with different bullet patterns and maybe even lasers.

Development may be even slower now that summer break is over, but I’ll still be working on it on the side.
